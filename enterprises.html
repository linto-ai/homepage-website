Project maintained by LINAGORA

The LinTO platform is an Open-Source toolbox that allows the development and integration of voice interaction processes that meet the needs of both professionals and the industry.

The research project is funded by the French government's PIA (Programme d'Investissement d'Avenir, for “Future Investment Program”) as part of the Grands Défis du Numérique. It units technological companies such as LINAGORA and ZELROS and research laboratories such as IRIT, LaaS, CNRS and the computer research laboratory of the Ecole Polytechnique.

Our business model

The Open Source system is made fully available under the GNU Affero licence, with no hidden costs, no license fees, no freemium model.

We deploy, specialize and maintain the solution at your place or for you (SaaS). We support your research and development work with high value-added information and perform specific developments for your use.

LinTO in your company

Modular client-server architecture and services

La plateforme est constituée de services indépendants et utilisables à la carte selon une logique client / serveur

The plateform is made of independant services and usable as you wich in a client / server logic

  • LinTO - Server: This component is the securised entry point for client system of your platform
  • LinTO - LinSTT: Speech To Text API. Ce composant réalise la transcription des audios au format texte, via une API
  • LinTO - NLU: Natural Language Understanding. This component evaluate the understanding of the natural language from a text file through its API
  • LinTO - Skills Server: Base on IBM "Node Red" engine, It enables the provision of skills for one or more LinTO customers in their business contexts

Service de connexion des clients LinTO: LinTO - Server

This component is the secure entry point for the client systems of your platform

Use the IoT MQTT standard to ensure real-time operation with perfect service quality

Any software with MQTT or WebSocket connectivity can establish the connection to your platform

Speech transcription service: LinTO - LinSTT

Foundation of our platform, this component transcribes audios provided from an API

  • Usable in streaming / audio files
  • Available for other workflows within the company, for example to add voice access to existing business applications or chatbots
  • Perfectly meets the industrial challenges of deployment and service quality with a solid scalable implementation on clusters and a management API
  • Transmits results in text format for short or long audio content
  • Allows the use of several AI models dedicated to specific use cases
  • Effectively transcribes proper names, business terms and provides context-specific formatting (dates, numbers, acronyms...)
  • Automatically learns according to your usage when adding or removing skills to your platform

Protocole dévaluation
  • Sélection de corpus
    • 30 minutes d'extrait audio avec leur transcription "verbatim" ont été sélectionnées pour les deux ensembles de parole lue et spontanée.
    • Les corpus ont été enregistré dans un environnement propre ("SNR ~= 20")
    • Le corpus parole lue a été enregistré par 8 locuteurs (4 hommes et 4 femmes) sur un journal français d'actualité
  • Calcul des performances et normalisation
    • Le calcul des performances a été évalué sur la transcription verbatim sans ponctuation, une normalisation des chiffres, dates, pourcentage.. etc a été effectué
    • Le taux d'erreur mot WER qui est reporté sur le tableau évalue le taux d'insertion, de suppression, ou de substitution de mot prédit par rapport à la vérité terrain. Plus la valeur est basse, plus le système est performant

Natural Language Understanding service: LinTO - NLU

This component evaluates and understands the natural language provided to its API in text format. It detects intentions, values entities and produces a JSON response that can then be used by the skills execution service.

  • Based on the SNCF Tock project (The Open Conversation Kit)
  • Supports other engines such as Rasa or Stanford NLP
  • Supports the use of several AI models dedicated to specific use cases
  • Automatically learns according to your usage when adding or removing skills to your platform

Skills Execution service: LinTO - Skill Server

Base on IBM's Node Red it allows the implementation of skills for one or more LinTO customers in their business contexts (a meeting room, a corridor or even as part of the management of a voice application).

  • Compatible with all existing Node Red skills base (more than 2000!)
  • Centralized configuration of [data bus systems], authentication information, etc.
  • Simple development of dedicated business skills with many examples provided - see documentation
  • Automatic re-training of AI models when deploying or deleting skills and dictionaries
  • Easy interoperability of skills with all your APIs or connected objects through visual workflow programming

LinTO Administration Interface: LinTO - Admin

Web application allowing to perform all the operations of configuration and maintenance of the platform.

  • Management of multiple contexts defining as many specific workflows using templates (Meeting Room, Application, etc.)
  • Visual editing of the workflow (adding/removing skills)
  • Updating management, exploitation of customer fleets
  • Monitoring temps réél des clients LinTO connectés
  • Real-time monitoring of connected LinTO customers
  • LinTO customer provisioning and deployment with a serial number embedding mechanism
  • Remote setting of sound level, network connectivity, languages, etc.

LinTO for Raspberry Pi & ARM systems

We currently offer a standard LinTO client based on the Raspberry Pi (or other ARM smart boards). This solution is an ideal demonstrator for your research and development work on automatic speech recognition in companies.

This solution is delivered as a ready-to-use solution (Raspberry kit, guides and download links) from our dedicated developer website.

For makers, hobbyists and research and development teams who want to free themselves from any external connection to GAFAM systems, we offer here the most advanced and state-of-the-art system for open-source voice assistant projects.

  • Customizable embedded Wake word(s) models
  • The entire voice processing chain controlled on clean infrastructures
  • Complete ARM system image creation tools based on the Qemu emulator
  • Rich and easily adaptable graphical interface

LinTO multiplateforme

La connexion d’un système client à la plateforme est simplifiée grâce à l’utilisation du composant LinTO - Server qui supporte le protocole standard de l’IoT, le MQTT. Cette connexion se met facilement en oeuvre dans un développement logiciel dédié à votre projet.

Le développement d’un client Android et Web, rationalisant l’expérience du client LinTO, constitue l’approche que nous privilégions dans notre roadmap produit.

Le client multiplateforme est actuellement en cours de développement et vise à répondre aux attentes de l’industrie quant au déploiement de la solution sans développements spécifiques. (Objectif de première version publique Q2 2020)

Assistant vocal Android SANS Google

De nombreuses initiatives existent pour "dé-googliser" les téléphones Android. (Lineage OS ou le projet /e/)

Ces initiatives s’appuient sur diverses API oogle (exemple).

Les notifications push, les cartes géographiques, le moteur de recherche... des alternatives existent, mais pas pour remplacer le fameux "Ok Google".

Beaucoup d’entreprises distribuent ainsi des produits Android professionnalisés grâce à ce type de distribution AOSP (Android Open Source Project) mais doivent faire l’impasse sur le pilotage vocal.

La plateforme LinTO vise à permettre enfin de mettre en oeuvre l’interactivité vocale dans un périphérique Android entièrement respectueux des données des utilisateurs.
